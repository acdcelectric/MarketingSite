CHARGING AS A SERVICE (CAAS)

EV Charging as a Service offers chance to the people who are interested to have EV charging in their sites.The investment involves paying a annual fee which is like a subscription . By paying the amount you can offer EV chargers to your customers, employees, or tenants .The ownership and maintenance of the EV charging stations are a third-party’s responsibility.

WHO IS THE OWNER OF THE STATIONS ?

The EV charging stations used as EV Charging as a Service will remain in the ownership of the EV CaaS provider. the EV CaaS user will be considered a host of the charging network. After the subscription period is over the things will be taken over by the owner. The EV CaaS host can extend the subscription and can upgrade their charging stations or can replace the charges with new models or different types.

BENEFITS OF CAAS

  • Lower Upfront Costs

    EV CaaS users only pays annual fee to the provider for the chargers.So No investment and no upfront costs.

  • Predictable Expenses

    EV CaaS provider can predict their income and expense and calculate their profit from the first day on.

  • Time and human resources involved in the project

    There is no need for additional hiring or additional time to be dedicated to the project because EV CaaS suppliers will supply the finished product and maintain everything.

  • Revenue generation

    Depending on the selected Charging as a Service business model, EV CaaS can generate revenue and help pay for the monthly or annual service fee.

  • Boosting the company’s public image

    If a company is socially responsible and sustainable, it can boost its public image and status among customers. So offering EV charging and promoting EV usage can only benefit the company.

  • Putting the company on the e-mobility map

    Having charging points at your property directly puts you in online EV Charging databases. With the EV community growing every second, being listed in EV Charging apps put the company on the map for EV users.


OPEN CHARGE POINT PROTOCOL

Similar to mobile phones and cell phone networks, the Open Charge Point Protocol (OCPP) is an application protocol for communication between Electric Vehicle (EV) charging stations and a central management system, often known as a charging station network.


BENEFITS OF OCPP

  • Charging station owners, or hosts, are less vulnerable to individual system suppliers – if a charging station manufacturer ceased to exist, the host could switch to another OCPP-based network.
  • Giving users of charging stations the freedom to choose and utilize any network on any charging station and through market forces encourage manufacturers of charging stations and network providers to compete on price, service, product features, and innovation, all of which would increase demand from charge station owners. The final outcome is a substantial benefit for EV drivers as the infrastructure for charging stations grows.
  • Since only one operating system is required, it is simpler to develop a large-scale, noticeable network that utilizes a variety of charging stations.
  • Ease interoperability
